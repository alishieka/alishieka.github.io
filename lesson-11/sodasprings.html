Soda Springs Idaho’s Geyser Park

Adjacent to the popular Oregon Trail, the city of Soda Springs, in the state of Idaho, has a unique attraction called the Soda Springs Geyser. The geographic region has been known for its geothermal activity for a long time. The geyser was discovered in 1937 during an effort to find water naturally for a swimming pool. The geyser was uncovered while drilling into a subterranean chamber that contained a mixture of water and carbon dioxide gas.

Today, the Soda Springs geyser goes off every hour on the hour and is controlled manually by the city. Access to view the geyser is free, but one may submit donations in the nearby museum. On average, this geyser normally propels water over 100 feet into the atmosphere with an estimated 2000 gallons of flowing every minute. The water temperature is around 72 degrees Fahrenheit.

Written by Brigham Young University
