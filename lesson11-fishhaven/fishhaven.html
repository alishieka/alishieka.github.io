Little Fish Haven, Idaho, is paradise for residents

Originally named Rush Creek, Fish Haven is a community along the shores of Bear Lake in Bear Lake County, Idaho, United States of America. It is 2.5 miles (4 km) north of the Utah border. It is a paradise undiscovered. Many people who drive here realize the raw beauty of a place known for being bordered by the shores of a vast natural lake and high desert mountains.

In wintertime, the hills outside this little paradise echo with the loud sounds of snow machines. Residents of Fish Haven say that population drops rapidly when things get cold. However, in the summer, thousands of tourists take to the lake's bright blue waters with fishing poles or water skis. There are grocery stores, restaurants, and gift shops in the town. The Bear Lake Regional Commission wants to draw persons to Fish Haven and to promote the entire Bear Lake valley.
