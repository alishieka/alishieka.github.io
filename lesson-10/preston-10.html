Festival of Lights

Preston, Idaho, is a little agricultural community in the northern area of Cache Valley. In the early times, Indians, who camped nearby, used it as a place to hunt. The first men to visit the region were immigrants, trappers, and explorers. Latter-day saint pioneers founded a community in that area and named it Worm Creek and was changed to Preston to honor William B. Preston.

Every year Preston celebrates the Idaho Festival of Lights which begins the day after Thanksgiving and goes until December 31. The festival was created by 2 local businessmen along with several other community organizations to assist in celebrating Christmas within the community. The festival has displays in the city park, a lighted parade, and fireworks. During the festival, the sellers along the main street of Preston have the front of their stores decorated with Christmas lights.
